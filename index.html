About Viginere Encryption

Vigenere encryption is a polyalphabetic substitution cipher where each letter in the plaintext is shifted according to a keyword,
creating a more complex and secure encryption than the simple Caesar cipher.
It uses a keyword to determine the amount of shift for each character,
allowing different parts of the message to be shifted by different amounts, making it harder to decrypt without the keyword.
The result is a more secure form of encryption compared to Caesar ciphers.

Let's walk through an example to understand how it works:

Suppose we have a plaintext message: "HELLO" and a keyword: "KEY." First, we repeat the keyword to match the length of the plaintext:
Plaintext: HELLO
Keyword: KEYKE Next, we assign a numerical value to each letter, where A=0, B=1, C=2, and so on:
HELLO -> 7 4 11 11 14 We also convert the keyword to numbers:
KEYKE -> 10 4 24 10 4 Now, we shift each letter of the plaintext by the corresponding letter in the keyword:
H -> 7 (H + K)
E -> 8 (E + E)
L -> 35 (L + Y)
L -> 21 (L + K)
O -> 18 (O + E) Finally, we convert the shifted numbers back to letters:
C -> 7 (A=0, B=1, C=2)
I -> 8
A -> 35 (Z=25, A=0, B=1, C=2)
K -> 11
S -> 18 So, the encrypted message is "CIAKS."
To decrypt the message, the recipient uses the same keyword to reverse the process and reveal the original plaintext, "HELLO."
